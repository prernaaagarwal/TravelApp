# The 30-second walkthrough — when you only get a minute

> Use when: elevator, end of a panel, intro at a dinner, investor says
> "I have 90 seconds, give me the headline." Memorize three versions.
> Pick the one that fits the time pressure.

## The 10-second version (the loglines)

Pick ONE of these three based on the room. None of these is the pitch —
it's the *opening that earns the next 20 seconds.*

### Logline A — Investor focus
> "Wander Women is the verified-contributor incident map for solo women
> traveling in India. Live product, 25 cards, 22 cities — pre-revenue,
> raising seed."

### Logline B — Product focus
> "Think TripAdvisor minus the anonymous noise — every safety report and
> intel card is from a named, verified woman. We launched the Beware
> Board in 22 cities."

### Logline C — Customer focus
> "We help solo women travel India safely with crowd-sourced incident
> data and verified intel cards from women who've actually been there.
> Live product, raising seed."

**Default:** Logline A in any investor room. Logline B in product/tech
rooms. Logline C in consumer/marketing rooms.

## The 30-second version (the walk-through)

This is the canonical script. Time it on your phone — 30 seconds means
~75 words at conversational pace. Practice 10 times.

> "Wander Women is the verified-contributor safety platform for solo
> women travelers — India first.
>
> Two surfaces drive the product: a Beware Board with date-stamped,
> geo-tagged incident reports across 22 cities, and Trip Intel Cards
> for 25 destinations, each authored by a named, ID-verified woman
> contributor.
>
> 36 of our 62 routes run on live Supabase backend with real auth.
> Payments and 100 cards launch with the round.
>
> Year-1 ARR target ₹15L; year-3 ₹3.6 Cr — base case, not moonshot."

**Time it.** If you finish in under 25 seconds, slow down. Over 35 seconds,
cut adjectives.

### Phrases that earn the next 30 seconds

If they're still listening, they'll cue you with one of these. Each has
a prepared 60-second extension:

- *"Tell me more about the Beware Board"* → city, count, moderation flow,
  defamation control
- *"How is this different from Reddit / TripAdvisor?"* → named attribution
  + verification + structured data, in 30 seconds
- *"What's the wedge?"* → "Indian women + safety + verified" — no
  apology, no ten-feature list
- *"What's your raise?"* → number, valuation range, milestones, dilution

## The 60-second version (when they say "you have a minute")

Keep the 30-second core, then add:

> "[30-second core above]
>
> The thesis: AI is killing generic travel content because ChatGPT
> answers 'is Goa safe' from training data. What it can't answer is
> 'what scams happened this month in Bangkok' — that's recency, that's
> verification, that's our data.
>
> Today we have 280 organic visitors a month, 8 named contributors, 100
> moderated Beware reports. We're underwriting growth, not pretending
> it's already happened. Round is ₹2.5 Cr seed at ₹15 Cr post for 18
> months of runway and 1,000 paid members + technical co-founder.
>
> Live product is at wanderwomen.in. Want me to walk you through it on
> your phone right now?"

**The closer matters.** The "want me to walk you through it on your
phone right now" line converts a hallway conversation into a 5-minute
demo. Investors love it because it costs them nothing and gives them
real signal.

## What NOT to do in 30 seconds

1. **Don't list features.** "We have intel cards, beware board, buddy
   matching, vault, feed, shop, contributor profiles..." — you've
   already lost them. Pick two surfaces, drop the rest.

2. **Don't say "the Uber for X" or "the Airbnb for Y."** Stretch
   comparables signal you don't have a real thesis.

3. **Don't say "we're disrupting X."** Disruption is what investors
   decide, not what founders claim.

4. **Don't say "TAM is huge."** Every founder says this. Either give a
   real number or skip TAM.

5. **Don't apologize.** "It's still early" / "the product is rough" —
   they'll see that themselves; pre-apologizing weakens you.

6. **Don't pitch the moonshot.** "We're going to be a $1B company" —
   the bigger the claim in 30 seconds, the less serious you look.
   Pitch the believable thing.

7. **Don't end with "what do you think?"** Ends the conversation. End
   with a question that earns the next 30 seconds: *"Want me to walk
   you through the live product?"*

## Practice protocol

1. Record yourself on phone voice memo
2. Time the recording — must be 28–32 seconds
3. Listen back: did you say any of the seven NOT items?
4. Re-record until you hit time and content cleanly 3 times in a row
5. Practice with one trusted person — they tell you what they
   remember after hearing it once. If they can repeat the loglines
   back, you've earned the pitch.

The test: if a friend can say "named contributors, India safety, live
product, raising seed" *unprompted* an hour after hearing your 30
seconds — your pitch is ready.

## Calibration over time

After every meeting, write down ONE question the investor asked in
the first minute. After 5 meetings you'll see the pattern. Update the
30-second script to *pre-empt* the most common question — that's how
the pitch sharpens.

Common patterns to expect:
- *"What's defensible?"* → emphasize verification + primary data
- *"What's the ARR?"* → say the year-1 number first
- *"Is it built?"* → "Yes, 62 routes live, 36 on Supabase backend"
- *"Why women-only?"* → safety + WTP + community trust
- *"Why now?"* → AI shifting channels + DPDP creating verification
  arbitrage

## When the room shifts

If the investor's body language says they want more detail (leaning
in, eye contact, taking notes) — switch from 30-second mode to
walk-through mode. Open the laptop, navigate to wanderwomen.in,
show the Beware Board for Goa first (most viscerally compelling),
then `/intel/goa-india` (the conversion surface), then `/contributor/
ananya-mumbai` (the trust signal).

If body language says they're done (looking at phone, glancing away) —
deliver the closer cleanly, hand them your card, and step away. Don't
press. Most investor opinions form in the first 90 seconds; pressing
beyond won't change them.

## End state

The goal of the 30-second pitch isn't to close the round. It's to
earn ONE of three responses:
1. *"Send me your deck."* (text-based diligence)
2. *"Can we put time on the calendar?"* (formal meeting)
3. *"You should talk to [specific person]."* (intro path)

Anything beyond those three is conversation noise. Optimize the script
for those three responses, not for elegance.
