# Quarterly investor update — letter template

> Send within 14 days of quarter-end to: angels, advisors, lead
> investor (when you have one), close customers/contributors.
>
> Length: one page. Read in 4 minutes. Skim in 30 seconds.
>
> Discipline: every quarter, even when there's nothing exciting.
> Especially when there's nothing exciting. Investors fund founders
> who report consistently — not founders who only show up at fundraise.

## Why this template exists

Most founders skip quarterly updates because:
1. They don't have enough good news
2. They feel awkward sharing bad news
3. They don't know the format

This template solves all three. The format is fixed, the bad-news
section is named explicitly so you can't avoid it, and "no news
quarter" is a valid update.

## Format

```
Subject: Wander Women — Q[X] [YYYY] update

Hi all,

[1-sentence theme of the quarter]

This quarter's highlights and lowlights, in numbers and in words.

THE NUMBERS
- Cards live: N (was M last quarter)
- Beware reports moderated: N this quarter (cumulative: M)
- New paid members: N (cumulative: M)
- Top-line MRR: ₹N (was ₹M last quarter)
- D30 retention this quarter: N% (last quarter M%)
- Press mentions: T1: N · T2: N · T3: N

WHAT WORKED
- [Specific thing 1, with metric attached]
- [Specific thing 2]
- [Specific thing 3]

WHAT DIDN'T WORK
- [Specific thing 1, with what you learned]
- [Specific thing 2]
- [The decision you'd reverse if you could]

DECISIONS WE MADE
- [Decision 1, in plain English, why made]
- [Decision 2]

WHAT'S NEXT
- Q[X+1] focus: [one-sentence theme]
- Top 3 priorities for Q[X+1]:
  1. [Priority 1, with target metric]
  2. [Priority 2]
  3. [Priority 3]

ASKS
[One specific ask. Not "any introductions"; instead "I'm raising a
₹2.5 Cr seed and I'd value an intro to [specific person]". Or
"I need a designer for two days of contract work — anyone you
recommend?"]

PERSONAL
[One sentence about how you're doing. Permission to say "tired" or
"struggling with X". Investors who get vulnerable updates trust
the founder more, not less.]

— [Founder]
[Personal email · Phone · LinkedIn]
```

## Variations

### "Slow quarter" version
If genuinely nothing big happened — say so. Investors trust founders
who name slowness. Mark the THE NUMBERS section with last-quarter-
delta, mark the WHAT WORKED section with one specific small win, and
write "WHAT DIDN'T WORK: pace. We're behind on cards-shipped target.
Here's the reset plan."

### "Crisis quarter" version
If the quarter was bad — legal threat, key person leaving, missed
milestone — front-load the bad news. *"This was a hard quarter.
Three things: (1) [bad thing 1], (2) [bad thing 2], (3) [bad thing 3].
What I'm doing about each: ..."* Investors who get bad news first
believe the good news that follows.

### "We raised" quarter
If you closed a round — note it but don't make it the headline. The
metrics that follow the round are the headline.

## Cadence

- **Q1 (April–June):** sent first week of July
- **Q2 (July–Sept):** sent first week of October
- **Q3 (Oct–Dec):** sent first week of January
- **Q4 (Jan–March):** sent first week of April

Block the calendar permanently. The discipline of the quarterly send
is what you're doing — content matters less than consistency.

## What gets attached

Either nothing (recommended) or one of:
- One screenshot of the cohort dashboard (not three)
- The deck if you've materially updated it
- A specific link to a recent press piece

Never attach a 30-page deep dive. The whole point is one-page brevity.

## What goes in the email body vs the BCC list

- **Body:** addressed "Hi all," with the email itself
- **To: yourself; BCC: investors and advisors.** Always BCC. Never let
  investors see each other's email addresses; that's how privacy
  complaints happen.
- **Personalize line:** if any single investor needs a personal
  preface, send a separate email for them, not a personalization
  inside the BCC

## Writing tips

- Numbers first, narrative second
- Short sentences. No paragraph longer than 4 sentences
- Don't apologize. Report.
- Don't bury bad news. Lead with it
- Use "we" sparingly when you're solo-founder; "I" is more honest
- Let the personal line be vulnerable. It's load-bearing for trust

## After sending

- File the update in `docs/investor/updates/<YYYY-Qn>-update.md`
  (you'll thank yourself in year 2 when you can re-read your own
  trajectory)
- Wait. Don't follow up. Investors who care will reply within a week
- If you get questions, reply individually — don't blast a clarification
