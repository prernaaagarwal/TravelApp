# Year-1 sensitivity table — capture × conversion = ARR

> Print this. Fold it in your pitch folder. Hand it across the table when
> the investor pushes on any single assumption.

## The math (all numbers verifiable)

**Constants for year 1:**
- 47 SEO surfaces (live cards + Beware Board cities)
- 1,000 monthly searches per surface (replace with verified midpoint)
- Total addressable: 47,000 searches/month = 564,000/year
- Email → paid conversion: 4% (held constant for this table)
- ARPU per paid member (year 1): ₹1,449

**Variables:**
- **Capture rate** — % of total addressable searches we win as visitors
- **Visitor → email conversion** — % of visitors who give us email

## The 3 × 3 grid

Rounded to whole members.

## The takeaway you walk in with

> "Even at our most pessimistic case — 2% capture and 2% conversion —
> total year-1 ARR is ₹5.9L. That's covered by the round. At base case,
> ₹15.7L. The model isn't load-bearing on heroic assumptions. It works at
> every cell of this table."

## Why this is the right answer to "your numbers feel optimistic"

Investors push on optimism because most decks collapse under one
unfavorable assumption swap. This grid says: *even at 2× downside on
both axes, the business still exists.* That's a defensibility statement,
not a forecast.

## What changes outside this grid

This table holds three things constant. If they change, redo the math:

1. **Surface count = 47.** If we ship 75 surfaces by year-end, the entire
   grid shifts up ~60%.
2. **Keyword volume = 1,000.** Verified midpoint may be higher. Re-run
   after `docs/strategy/keyword-research.md`.
3. **Email → paid conversion = 4%.** This is the most uncertain number;
   if it's actually 2%, every cell halves.

A second grid (year 2 or year 3) is upside, not base. Don't bring it to
the meeting unless the investor specifically asks.
