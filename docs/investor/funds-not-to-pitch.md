# Funds we should and shouldn't pitch

> Pre-empts 20 cold pitches that are pre-determined no's. Funds have
> theses. Many funds will pass on us before they've finished reading
> the deck — not because we're bad, but because we don't fit. Save
> the cold-outreach budget for warm fits.
>
> **Honesty marker:** fund theses change every 12–18 months. Verify
> currently active partners and current thesis before pitching. Use
> Crunchbase, Tracxn, or each fund's website (newer than this doc).

## How to read this doc

- **Strong fit** — pitch with conviction, expect honest engagement
- **Worth a try** — fit is plausible but not certain; warm intro
  recommended over cold
- **Bad fit** — don't pitch; save the slot
- **Avoid** — actively bad reasons (conflicts, prior portfolio, thesis
  mismatch)

The reasoning under each entry matters more than the bucket.

## Bad fit (don't waste meetings)

### Sequoia India / Surge / Peak XV
- **Why bad:** seed allocation has shifted; their seed thesis now
  bias toward consumer scale + India-export plays. Travel-content
  specifically isn't a priority area in 2026.
- **Exception:** if they explicitly reach out to you, take the
  meeting. Otherwise don't cold pitch.

### Flipkart Ventures / Walmart Innovation
- **Why bad:** strategic VC for commerce. Travel-content is too far
  from their parent's priorities.

### Khosla Ventures India / DST Global
- **Why bad:** US-headquartered, India-allocations are large-cheque
  growth-stage only. Not a seed conversation.

### Tiger Global / SoftBank Vision Fund
- **Why bad:** scale-stage only. ₹500 Cr+ rounds, not seed.

### Any "AI-first" or "AI-thesis" fund
- **Why bad:** we use AI as an assistive tool, not as the product.
  Pitching us as an AI company misrepresents and they'll pass.

## Avoid (active conflicts)

### Funds with travel-platform portfolio companies
Check Crunchbase before pitching ANY fund: do they already have
TripAdvisor competitors, Lonely Planet competitors, or women-only
platform investments?

Likely conflicts (verify currently):
- **MakeMyTrip's strategic arm** — they own MMT; we'd compete with
  bookings. Don't pitch.
- **Yatra Online** — same logic
- **Booking Holdings strategic** — same
- **Anyone who funded a JustGlobetrotting / Sisterhood Travel /
  women-only-travel company** — likely a portfolio conflict; if
  they took the deal there, they probably can't fund us
  competitively

### Funds where the partner has expressed public skepticism of
women-only platforms

Some VCs have publicly argued (on Twitter, podcasts) against women-
only platforms as "exclusionary" or "small TAM". Don't pitch them —
you'll spend the meeting defending the basic premise instead of
discussing the deal. Note specific names you've encountered as you
research and add to this list privately.

## Funds whose passes are valuable

Some passes give you specific information:

  push for the technical co-founder hire

A pass with specific feedback is worth more than a token cheque.

## How to research a new fund (15 min)

Before adding any fund to your "pitch list":

1. **Crunchbase** — recent investments in consumer / travel / women /
   community / content; thesis match within last 18 months
2. **Tracxn** (India-focused) — same; better Indian coverage
3. **The fund's website** — explicit thesis statement; portfolio
4. **Twitter / LinkedIn of partners** — active engagement, recent
   posts; what they say in public about deals
5. **Their portfolio founders on Twitter** — do they recommend the
   fund? are there portfolio companies that mirror our profile?
6. **News in last 90 days** — fund-specific news, partner-level news
7. **The previous-round investor in their portfolio company** — if a
   portfolio company raised at Series A, who led? That tells you
   who they typically pre-empt.

If 4+ of these checks come up positive, it's a genuine fit. 2-3 = warm
intro recommended; 0-1 = don't bother.

## Pitch sequence

Recommended order to maximize signal:

1. **Week 1–2:** Better Capital + 1-2 angels (low stakes, fast feedback)
2. **Week 3:** Stellaris + India Quotient (mission-aligned, sympathetic)
3. **Week 4–5:** Blume + 3one4 (mainstream consumer)
4. **Week 6:** Lightspeed / Accel / Kalaari (selectively)
5. **Throughout:** angels via LetsVenture + IAN + direct outreach

Why this order: the early funds give you specific signal you use to
sharpen the pitch by the time you're talking to the bigger funds.
You don't waste your best shot on Day 1.
