# 60-second Loom walkthrough — script + recording protocol

> The Loom embedded in a follow-up email lifts response rates 3×.
> A 60-second Loom that shows the product is the single best
> investor-facing artifact you can produce. Total recording time:
> ~30 minutes including 4–6 takes.

## Why 60 seconds, not longer

Investors have 90 seconds for any unsolicited Loom. Every second past
that is dropped. 60 seconds of tight product walkthrough is the right
size: enough to show, short enough to watch.

If you cannot say what's compelling about the product in 60 seconds,
you don't yet know what's compelling about the product. The discipline
of the 60-second cut is the discipline of pitch clarity.

## Setup (one-time, ~15 min)

1. **Tool:** loom.com (free tier supports unlimited videos under 5 min)
2. **Recording quality:** HD, 1080p
3. **Browser:** Chrome incognito window — no extensions, no
   bookmarks visible, no other tabs
4. **Window size:** 1280×800 (matches what investors see on their
   laptops)
5. **Tabs prepared in advance, in this order:**
   - Tab 1: `wanderwomen.in` (landing)
   - Tab 2: `wanderwomen.in/intel/goa-india` (flagship card)
   - Tab 3: `wanderwomen.in/community/beware/goa-india` (Beware Board)
   - Tab 4: `wanderwomen.in/contributor/[name]` (contributor profile)
6. **Microphone test:** record 10 seconds, listen back, fix audio
   levels before doing the real take

## The script (60 seconds, ~150 words)

Read this aloud at conversational pace. Time yourself. Should hit
58–62 seconds. If you're at 50, slow down. If you're at 70, cut.

```
[Open on Tab 1: wanderwomen.in landing]

[0:00–0:08]
"Hi — I'm [your name], founder of Wander Women. We're the verified-
contributor incident map for solo women travelers in India.

[0:08–0:20]
[Click into Tab 2: /intel/goa-india]
This is a Trip Intel Card for Goa — written by Riya, an ID-verified
woman who's traveled solo there 8 times. You can see the scams,
the neighborhood ratings, the budget. Every claim is dated and
attributed.

[0:20–0:40]
[Switch to Tab 3: /community/beware/goa-india]
This is the Beware Board for Goa — incident reports submitted by
verified women, geo-tagged, moderated within 36 hours. This is
defensible primary data. ChatGPT can't generate this.

[0:40–0:55]
[Switch to Tab 4: contributor profile]
And every contributor has a public profile — verified identity,
trip history, badge. Eight named contributors today, growing
to a paid network of 50 in 90 days post-funding.

[0:55–1:00]
[Switch back to Tab 1]
We're at wanderwomen.in. Happy to walk through any of this in
more detail."

[End on Tab 1 landing]
```

## Recording protocol

### Take 1 — full read

Record the script straight through. Don't pause. Don't redo. Just
get one full take captured.

### Take 2 — natural

Watch take 1. Note the awkward bits. Re-record without the script in
front of you. Talk about each surface as you click into it.

### Take 3 — tight

Watch take 2. Cut anything that's redundant. Re-record at faster pace
with sharper transitions.

### Take 4 — final

Take 3 with one polish:
- Smile at the start (audible in voice)
- Crisp click between tabs
- End on a clean pause, not "uh okay that's it"

Most founders are at take 6 before they have a good one. Plan for
that.

## Common mistakes to avoid

### "Let me show you a few things"
The Loom IS showing them things. Don't narrate the act of showing.

### Reading the screen
"Here you can see scams, neighborhoods, transport, budget..." —
the investor sees this. Tell them what's NOT visible: who wrote it,
when, why it matters.

### Apologizing
"It's still rough", "this isn't fully finished", "ignore the
placeholder" — never. The investor sees what they see. Don't
preemptively explain it away.

### Slow zoom-ins
Don't zoom in on details. The investor will pause if they want to
read. Zoom-ins eat time.

### Mouse movements
Don't wave the cursor around. Click decisively, then talk.

### Background sound
Door opening, dog barking, traffic — record again. The Loom is the
investor's only sense of who you are. They will judge you on it.

### Filler words
"So", "um", "kind of", "basically" — script and rehearse to remove
these. Each filler word costs trust.

### Reading from notes
Investors can hear you reading. Memorize the script, then talk
naturally over the screens.

## After recording

1. **Watch it once at 1.5× speed.** If it still makes sense at fast
   forward, it's well-paced.
2. **Watch it once with sound off.** If the visual flow tells the
   story without audio, you've nailed the click sequence.
3. **Send it to one trusted founder/advisor.** Ask: "Can you tell me
   in one sentence what this product does after watching?" If they
   can repeat the wedge, you're done.
4. **Do not send to non-founder friends for feedback.** They'll
   focus on visual polish you can't change.

## Distribution

Once recorded:

1. **Set Loom privacy** to "anyone with link can view"
2. **Get the embed code** — Loom provides MP4 + iframe + thumbnail
3. **Embed in follow-up emails** as a clickable thumbnail (more
   click-throughs than a plain URL)
4. **Add to data room index** as a top-level link
5. **Pin to LinkedIn** if you're publicly fundraising (private
   distribution otherwise)

## Variations

### 30-second hyper-cut
For cold outreach where you don't yet have permission to send a
60-second video. Same script, 50% cut. Show only the Beware Board.

### 90-second tech walkthrough
For technical-partner archetype investors. Add 30 seconds at the
end:

```
[Tab 5: /llms.txt or /api/mcp]
"For technical investors — we ship llms.txt and a public MCP
endpoint. AI agents can query our intel directly. Citable, agent-
ready, structured."
```

### 3-minute deep dive
Only on request. Adds: methodology page, admin cohorts dashboard
(via screen share, redacted), demo of submitting a Beware report.

## Refreshing the Loom

Re-record when:
- The product visual changes materially (new design, new feature)
- Numbers in the script become stale (contributor count, etc.)
- Quarterly, even if nothing changed (proves freshness)

Don't re-record for:
- Minor copy changes
- Bug fixes
- Test users joining

## Testing the Loom in your follow-up email

Before sending the Loom in production emails:

1. Send to yourself first
2. Watch on your phone (most investors check email on phone)
3. Verify the thumbnail looks right
4. Verify audio plays without explicit play-button click
5. If anything looks off, re-record before sending to investors

## Why the Loom is the secret weapon

In the founder community, Looms are mostly missing from follow-up
emails. When you send one, you stand out — not because you're
showing more, but because you're showing the *minimum effective
dose* of trust. A 60-second video says: *I've thought about your
time. I've practiced this. I have a real product.*

That's the asymmetric edge.
