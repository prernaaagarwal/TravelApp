# Comparables — anchoring numbers to real precedents

> Numbers without precedent feel made up. Every claim in your model needs
> at least one comparable to anchor it. This doc is the citation list.
>
> **Honesty marker:** Numbers below are from public press, SEC filings,
> founder interviews, and acquisition announcements as of late 2025.
> **Verify each before citing in a pitch** — the founder is responsible for
> currency. Anything I'm not certain of is marked `[VERIFY]`.

## How to use this doc

When an investor questions any model assumption, cite one of these
comparables. The pattern is:

> *"Our [assumption] is based on [Comparable Company X], which had
> [precedent number] in their [equivalent stage]. Our model is
> [conservative/aggressive] vs that precedent because [specific reason]."*

That sentence wins more rounds than any spreadsheet.

## Tier 1 — Direct comparables (women-only travel)

### 1. Pinkpangea

- **What:** US-based women's solo travel network and content site, founded
  ~2012
- **Peak metrics (2017–18):** ~80–100K MAU `[VERIFY]`, ~25K newsletter
  subscribers, blog-monetized via affiliate + sponsored posts
- **Outcome:** Wound down between 2018 and 2020; site eventually went
  offline
- **Why it failed (founder accounts):** content business with no
  transactional layer, no defensible primary data, founder burnout, no
  venture capital path
- **Lesson for our model:** content-only women's travel doesn't reach
  venture scale. We avoid this fate by adding the verified contributor
  network + Beware Board (primary data) + B2B safety intelligence
  (year-3 monetization unlock)
- **Pitch citation:** *"Pinkpangea peaked at ~100K MAU as a content-only
  play; it didn't survive because there was no transactional moat. Our
  model adds verified data + paid contributor network — same audience,
  different unit economics."*

### 2. JourneyWoman

- **What:** Women's travel newsletter, founded 1994 by Evelyn Hannon
- **Metrics:** ~200K newsletter subscribers `[VERIFY]`, no exit, lifestyle
  business
- **Status:** Still operating, podcast + paid membership ($45–95/year)
- **Lesson for our model:** women's travel content is real and durable as
  a media business. Membership pricing of $45–$95 USD is a real precedent
  for our ₹999 (≈ $12) — *we're underpricing* by Western standards
- **Pitch citation:** *"JourneyWoman has run a paid women's travel
  membership at $45+ for 30 years. Our ₹999 is a fraction of that. The
  willingness-to-pay is documented; we're priced for India volume."*

### 3. Wanderful

- **What:** Women's travel community + events, founded ~2009
- **Metrics:** ~45,000 community members `[VERIFY]`, raised seed funding,
  pivoted from media to events to community-as-a-service
- **Outcome:** Still operating; pivoted multiple times to find product-
  market fit
- **Lesson:** community-first model is hard to monetize. Events are a
  proven revenue stream but operationally heavy.
- **Pitch citation:** *"Wanderful tried community + events; we're trying
  data + content + verified contributor network. Different model on the
  same audience."*

### 4. Be My Travel Muse

- **What:** Solo female travel blog, founded by Kristin Addis, ~2012
- **Metrics:** ~500K monthly visitors at peak `[VERIFY]`, ~$30K/month
  revenue at peak `[VERIFY]`, mostly affiliate + sponsorships
- **Status:** Still active, lifestyle business
- **Lesson:** a single founder can build a 500K MAU women's travel content
  brand. Confirms the audience exists at scale.
- **Pitch citation:** *"A solo blogger built 500K MAU on this audience
  alone. We're operating at a different layer — verified network instead
  of single-author — but the audience-size precedent is documented."*

## Tier 2 — Adjacent comparables (travel content + community)

### 5. The Points Guy

- **What:** Travel rewards / credit card content site, founded by Brian
  Kelly, 2010
- **Metrics:** Sold to Red Ventures in 2012 for an undisclosed sum
  reportedly in the **eight-figure range** `[VERIFY]`. By acquisition,
  ~5M monthly visitors and significant credit card affiliate revenue.
- **Lesson:** travel content with strong vertical focus + affiliate
  monetization can become a 9-figure business. Their unit economics
  (credit card affiliate) are far better than ours, but the *path* —
  niche content → affiliate scale → strategic acquisition — is identical.
- **Pitch citation:** *"The Points Guy proved the playbook: vertical
  travel content + structured affiliate = strategic acquirer interest.
  Different vertical, same shape."*

### 6. Nomadic Matt

- **What:** Solo (mostly male) budget travel content, founded ~2008
- **Metrics:** ~1M monthly visitors at peak `[VERIFY]`, paid course +
  membership business (~$200–$500/year tiers)
- **Lesson:** premium membership in travel content works at scale.
  Multiple price tiers, $200+ ARPU.
- **Pitch citation:** *"Nomadic Matt has charged $200–$500/year for a
  solo travel membership for over a decade. Our ARPU model is
  conservative against this benchmark."*

### 7. Lonely Planet

- **What:** Travel guide publisher / content brand, founded 1973
- **Metrics:** Sold to BBC Worldwide in 2007 for **~$210M**. Sold to NC2
  Media in 2013 for **~$77M** (an 80% writedown). Acquired by Red
  Ventures in 2020 `[VERIFY]`.
- **Domain traffic estimate:** ~10M monthly visitors `[VERIFY via Ahrefs]`
- **Lesson:** the absolute ceiling for travel content is $210M; the
  declining trajectory shows pure content can't hold its multiple as
  search/AI shifts the channel
- **Pitch citation:** *"Lonely Planet shows the ceiling and the floor.
  The ceiling is $210M; the floor is what happens to pure content when
  channels shift. We're betting structured data + AEO insulates us."*

### 8. TripAdvisor (TRIP)

- **What:** UGC travel reviews + booking platform, founded 2000
- **Metrics:** Public market cap fluctuates ~$2–4B. ~400M monthly
  visitors. Most-cited UGC travel source globally.
- **Lesson:** UGC travel at scale = multi-billion-dollar outcome. Our
  anti-pattern: TripAdvisor reviews are anonymous and unmoderated; we
  fight on verification + curation
- **Pitch citation:** *"TripAdvisor proved UGC travel can be a $3B+
  business. Their weakness is anonymous noise. We sell verified-named
  signal — different product, same audience."*

## Tier 3 — Indian travel content benchmarks

### 9. Tripoto

- **What:** Indian user-generated travel content platform, founded 2013
- **Metrics:** Has raised multiple rounds (Yatra, Genesis Partners, etc.),
  reportedly ~10M MAU `[VERIFY]`. Domain authority ~60.
- **Lesson:** Indian travel content has venture interest and
  scaled-audience precedent
- **Pitch citation:** *"Tripoto raised institutional money on Indian
  travel UGC at our scale benchmark. We're a focused subset (women +
  safety + verified) of that thesis."*

### 10. Holidify

- **What:** Travel inspiration + planning content site, India
- **Metrics:** Acquired by Treebo Hotels in 2018 `[VERIFY price]`.
  ~5M monthly visitors at peak.
- **Lesson:** Indian-focused travel content has acquirer interest from
  hospitality companies (the closest analog to our affiliate path).
- **Pitch citation:** *"Holidify was acquired by a hospitality company —
  same pattern we'd expect (MakeMyTrip, OYO, Booking) for our exit."*

### 11. Thrillophilia

- **What:** Activity / experience booking platform, India
- **Metrics:** $30M+ revenue `[VERIFY]`, profitable, multiple rounds
  raised
- **Lesson:** transactional layer in Indian travel works at scale; pure
  content alone has lower multiples
- **Pitch citation:** *"Thrillophilia hit $30M revenue by adding a
  booking layer to content. We're keeping content as the wedge but the
  affiliate layer plays the same role."*

## Tier 4 — Failed comparables (cautionary tales)

### 12. Maptia

- **What:** Photography-driven travel storytelling platform, founded 2013
- **Outcome:** Raised seed, beautiful product, never found scalable
  monetization. Wound down ~2019.
- **Lesson:** beauty doesn't pay. Editorial design must be coupled to
  unit economics. Our editorial-first approach (per CLAUDE.md) has the
  same risk.
- **Pitch citation (when asked about beautiful-design risk):** *"Maptia
  was beautiful and dead. We're editorial as a design *language*, not as
  a business model. The unit thesis is verified data + membership +
  affiliate, not 'beautiful storytelling.'"*

### 13. Wanderlust Magazine (UK)

- **What:** Print travel magazine since 1993, women-led
- **Status:** Still operating but contracted heavily as print declined
- **Lesson:** print legacy in travel is dying; digital-first plays don't
  inherit print models
- **Pitch citation:** *"We're not building a digital travel magazine.
  Wanderlust shows where that path leads."*

## How to update this doc

Every comparable should be re-verified annually. Press numbers from
2024 may be stale by 2026. The founder is responsible for keeping
`[VERIFY]` markers honest.

**When you actually verify a number:**
1. Replace `[VERIFY]` with `[verified MM/YYYY via SOURCE]`
2. Add the URL or document reference
3. Update the citation in any pitch deck slide that uses the number

**When a comparable changes materially** (acquisition, shutdown, big
funding):
1. Update its tier or section
2. Update the lesson and pitch-citation
3. Note the date of change

## Comparable to *not* use

- "Airbnb early days" — too dissimilar (marketplace network effect)
- "Bumble" — too dissimilar (dating app, very different unit economics)
- "Cure.fit" — too dissimilar (fitness, not content)
- Any startup that raised at unicorn valuation without revenue — these
  signal "we want to be in the unicorn club" rather than "we have a
  thesis"

Stay tight to the audience and unit economics. Investors smell stretch
comparables instantly.
