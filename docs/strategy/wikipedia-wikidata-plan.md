# Wikipedia + Wikidata seeding — year-1 trust-building plan

> Goal: get Wander Women into Wikidata by month 6 and Wikipedia by month
> 12. Both are heavily weighted by every major LLM (Claude, GPT, Gemini,
> Perplexity all train on or cite Wikipedia). Don't try to file too
> early — premature filings get rejected and damage your future
> credibility.

## The strategic context

LLMs cite **Wikipedia first, Wikidata second, everything else third.** A
Wikipedia mention is worth more than 100 SEO backlinks for AEO purposes.
But Wikipedia is a community-edited encyclopedia with strict notability
guidelines — you cannot self-serve into it. You earn it.

The reverse strategy: build the citation evidence Wikipedia editors need
to write your article. Then a third-party editor (or you, with full
disclosure) creates the article.

## The two systems

### Wikidata
- Structured database, easier to enter
- More forgiving notability rules
- Separate from Wikipedia (though linked)
- LLMs use it for entity disambiguation
- **Filing here first is recommended** — Wikipedia editors often check
  Wikidata before approving Wikipedia entries

### Wikipedia
- Notoriously strict notability (WP:NCORP for organizations)
- Requires "significant coverage in independent reliable secondary
  sources" — not press releases, not the company's own content
- Editors can decline articles that read promotional
- A bad-quality article gets deleted and a record of rejection lasts
- **File only when ready** — premature filing kills your future chances

## The notability ladder — where you must be before filing

Wikipedia's notability guideline (WP:GNG / WP:NCORP) requires:

1. **Coverage in multiple independent reliable sources** — not your
   blog, not press releases, not interviews where you're the source
2. **Sources are mainstream news** — Forbes, BBC, NYT, Mint, Bloomberg,
   ET, regional papers like The Hindu
3. **Coverage is non-trivial** — articles ABOUT the company, not just
   passing mentions

### Pre-filing checklist (you must hit ALL of these)

- [ ] At least 5 independent press articles, each ABOUT Wander Women
      (not just mentioning the founder or featuring you in a list of 10)
- [ ] At least 2 articles in Tier-1 publications (BBC, Bloomberg,
      Forbes India, ET, Mint, Hindustan Times, Business Insider)
- [ ] Coverage spans at least 6 months (one-week press cycle is not
      enough)
- [ ] At least one feature-length piece (1,000+ words) — not a blurb
- [ ] No "interview with founder" pieces alone — Wikipedia treats
      interviews as primary sources, not independent ones

If you can't honestly check all 5 boxes, **do not file yet.**

## Wikidata — the month-6 task

Wikidata is faster, easier, and an excellent prerequisite to Wikipedia.

### How to file (60 minutes)

1. **Create a Wikidata account** at <https://www.wikidata.org/>
2. **Disclose the conflict of interest** in your user profile if filing
   yourself — required by Wikidata's COI policy
3. **Search for existing entries first** — make sure no Wander Women
   entity already exists
4. **Create a new item** via "Create a new item"
5. **Fill the core properties:**

   ```
   Label (English): Wander Women
   Description: Indian women-only solo travel intelligence platform
   Aliases: Wander Women Travel; WanderWomen
   ```

6. **Add statements (key properties to fill):**

   - `instance of (P31)` → travel content company OR online platform
   - `country (P17)` → India
   - `inception (P571)` → 2026 (or your founding year)
   - `headquarters location (P159)` → [your city]
   - `official website (P856)` → https://wanderwomen.in
   - `founded by (P112)` → [founder name; create founder Wikidata entity if needed]
   - `industry (P452)` → travel; women's safety
   - `description from source (P973)` → link to your best press article

7. **Add references** for every statement — link to a press article
   that confirms the fact. **No reference = the statement gets removed.**

### After filing
- The entity may take 24–72 hours to be indexed
- Other Wikidata editors may suggest improvements; respond promptly
- Search for "Wander Women" on Wikidata to confirm

## Wikipedia — the month-12 task

This is the harder, higher-stakes task.

### Two filing paths

**Path A — Articles for Creation (WP:AfC) — RECOMMENDED**
- File the article as a draft
- A Wikipedia editor reviews it and either accepts, rejects, or
  comments
- Iterate based on feedback
- Lower risk; iterate to acceptance

**Path B — Direct creation in mainspace**
- Anyone can create a Wikipedia article directly
- High deletion risk if notability is borderline
- Reserve for when notability is unambiguous

**Always use Path A unless you have 10+ Tier-1 press articles.**

### How to file via WP:AfC

1. **Create a Wikipedia account** (use a personal account; don't
   create a brand-name account — it gets blocked)
2. **Disclose the conflict of interest** prominently in your user
   profile and on the article talk page (paid contribution disclosure
   is mandatory under Wikipedia's terms of service)
3. **Find a Wikipedia editor with relevant subject expertise** to
   review the draft before submission. Try:
   - WikiProject:Companies
   - WikiProject:Travel and tourism
   - WikiProject:Feminism
4. **Draft the article** with:

   ```
   == Lead paragraph ==
   What it is, when founded, where based. 2–3 sentences.

   == History ==
   Founding context, growth milestones. Cite press for each claim.

   == Product ==
   What the platform does — Trip Intel Cards, Beware Board,
   contributor verification, methodology. Cite the methodology page.

   == Reception ==
   What press said about the product. Quote independently.

   == See also ==
   Related Wikipedia articles (women's safety platforms, travel
   technology, Indian startups)

   == References ==
   Numbered list of every press article cited.
   ```

5. **Submit via Articles for Creation**

### Common rejection reasons (avoid these)

- Sources are press releases or interviews → use independent reporting
- Article reads promotional → keep it neutral, factual
- Founder bio mixed in → the article is about the company, not the
  founder
- Insufficient depth — 200 words gets rejected; aim for 800+
- Recent press only — sustained coverage matters
- Self-published sources (your own blog, your founder's LinkedIn) →
  remove

## What to do BEFORE month 6

These build the citation base. Without them, the month-6 Wikidata and
month-12 Wikipedia filings will fail.

### Press strategy (months 2–9)

1. **Identify 12 target publications** in three tiers:
   - Tier 1 (4): Mint, ET, Forbes India, BusinessLine
   - Tier 2 (4): YourStory, Inc42, MoneyControl, Tech in Asia
   - Tier 3 (4): The Better India, BehindWoods, NDTV, Outlook

2. **Pitch one story per quarter** to two publications each. Different
   angles per quarter:
   - Q1: founder story + product launch
   - Q2: women's safety in Indian travel (data-driven)
   - Q3: AI verification + DPDP compliance
   - Q4: milestone (members, contributors, exit data)

3. **Get one feature-length piece (1500+ words)** in months 4–6. This
   is the most-cited article in your future Wikipedia entry.

4. **Document every press mention** in `docs/press/mentions.md` with
   URL, date, publication, and a one-line summary.

### Conference / podcast presence (months 3–9)

- Speak at 2 industry conferences (TechSparks, Asia TravelTech, etc.)
- Be a guest on 4 podcasts (Indian startup podcasts, women's safety
  podcasts, travel podcasts)
- These create transcripts that AI engines train on, even if not
  Wikipedia-citable

### Industry recognition (months 6–12)

- Apply for Inc42 Women in Tech, ET Now Women's Leadership Awards
- Submit to category lists (Forbes 30 Under 30, etc.)
- Even being shortlisted creates citable coverage

## What NOT to do

- **Do not pay for Wikipedia placement.** Paid Wikipedia editing is
  banned and detected. You will be banned permanently.
- **Do not create a Wikipedia article using a corporate account.**
  Wikipedia auto-flags brand-named accounts.
- **Do not file with sponsored or advertorial sources.** Wikipedia
  editors check; sponsored content is excluded.
- **Do not file too early.** Rejection is a record that follows the
  topic. Wait until you'd succeed.

## Quarterly review

Every quarter, ask:
1. Did we add new press mentions this quarter?
2. Is the press independent enough for Wikipedia?
3. Are we on track for the month-6 Wikidata filing?
4. Are we on track for the month-12 Wikipedia filing?

If any answer is no — adjust the press strategy. The Wikipedia entry
is a year-long project, not a sprint. Treat it as one.
