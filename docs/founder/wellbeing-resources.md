# Founder wellbeing — resources and cadence

> Audience: the founder. Private. Not for the data room.
>
> The biggest risk to this product is founder burnout, not market risk or
> technical risk. This file lists concrete resources and a cadence you can
> hold yourself to. Update as you find things that work.
>
> **Why a doc and not just a habit:** when you're spiralling, you don't
> remember the resources. Open this file. Click one link.

## Why this matters specifically for THIS product

Wander Women is emotionally heavy:

- The Beware Board exposes you to descriptions of harassment, scams, and
  occasionally assault — multiple times per week as moderator
- Building a women's safety product while being a woman in tech in India
  is its own load
- Solo founder = no one to share the cognitive load with daily
- The product is adjacent to your own travel experiences; it's personal

This doc takes that seriously. It's not generic founder-self-care — it's
calibrated for the specific weight you're carrying.

## Therapist — find one, see them weekly

### Why weekly, not "as needed"

"As needed" becomes "never" once you're busy. Weekly is non-negotiable.
Move it to fortnightly only after 6 months of weekly is consistent.

### Where to find one (India-based, online + in-person)

| Service | What | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MindPeers** | Online therapy platform | ₹1,500–3,000/session | Filter for trauma-informed therapists |
| **Manastha** | Online therapy | ₹1,200–2,500/session | Strong on women's specific concerns |
| **TheLiveLoveLaughFoundation Helpline** | Free crisis | ₹0 | 9152987821 — for crisis only, not regular therapy |
| **iCall** | Free counseling helpline | ₹0 | 9152987821, M–Sat 8am–10pm |
| **YourDost** | Online therapy + chat | ₹500–2,500/session | Lower-cost entry point |
| **Direct** | Find a therapist via referral | ₹1,500–4,000/session | Ask 3 founder friends for therapist names; word-of-mouth quality is highest |

### What to look for in a therapist

- **Trauma-informed** — even if you don't think you need trauma work,
  women's-safety adjacent products bring up secondary exposure
- **Familiar with founder/entrepreneur stress** — bonus, not required
- **Not a "tough love" therapist** — you have enough toughness; you need
  a witness, not a coach
- **Available weekly at a consistent time** — schedule consistency matters

### When to switch therapists

After 4 sessions, ask yourself: *"Do I feel better leaving sessions
than I do going in?"* If no, switch. There is no reward for sticking
with a bad fit.

## Founder peer groups

### Why two groups, not one

One industry-mixed (peers across categories — keeps perspective).
One travel/women's-product specific (people who get the specific work).

### Industry-mixed (recommended starting points — verify currently active)

| Group | What | Cost | How to join |
|---|---|---|---|
| **WomenWhoStartUp** | India-based women founders | Free + paid tiers | wwsindia.com |
| **Founders for Founders** | Cross-stage Indian founders | Free | foundersforfounders.in |
| **Indian Angel Network founder community** | If/when you raise | Free for portfolio | Auto-included with IAN funding |
| **All Raise India** | Women in venture/startups | Free | allraise.org/india |
| **TheStartupScene** | India founder slack | Free | thestartupscene.in |

### Travel / women's product specific (smaller, harder to find — DM)

These are smaller communities, often DM-based, not formal:

- **Indian women travel creators** — DM groups around 200–500 people; ask
  Kanika at JustGlobetrotting or Shivya at TheShootingstar for an intro
- **Women in safety tech** — international Slack, find via SafetyDetectives
  or RightToBeApp
- **Women-only travel platform operators** — Wanderful (Beth Santos), Damesly,
  Solo Female Travelers Network — DM founders directly

Don't wait to be invited to these. DM directly. Founders help founders;
the worst that happens is no reply.

### Cadence

- **Industry-mixed group:** monthly call, 60 min. Hold this even when
  you don't think you need it. Especially when you don't think you
  need it.
- **Travel-specific group:** ad-hoc (3–6 calls/year). Lower cadence,
  higher specificity.

### What to talk about

NOT pitch practice. NOT roadmap reviews. The point of a peer group is
to talk about the *experience* of being a founder, not the company:

- The week you spent moderating beware reports and felt heavy
- The investor meeting that went poorly and what you learned
- The hard call you avoided making
- The moments you doubted whether to keep going

If you find yourself pitching to your peer group, you're using them
wrong. Stop, breathe, switch to feelings.

## Body + sleep — the operations layer

You can do without therapy for a few weeks. You cannot do without
sleep. Treat sleep as load-bearing infrastructure.

### Non-negotiables

- 7+ hours of sleep, 5+ nights per week
- Phone out of the bedroom (or in airplane mode in a drawer)
- No moderation work after 9 PM. The Beware Board waits.
- One full day per week with no Wander Women work. Anywhere. Even when
  it feels critical.

### Diagnostics (catch decline early)

If 3+ of these are true for 7+ days, escalate (therapist, doctor,
peer group, or a real break):

- [ ] Sleeping less than 6 hours most nights
- [ ] Skipping meals or eating only takeaway
- [ ] Avoiding the moderation queue when it's not full
- [ ] Avoiding investor emails/calls
- [ ] Feeling "behind" regardless of progress
- [ ] Numbness or constant low-grade anxiety
- [ ] Feeling like Wander Women is the only thing about you
- [ ] Telling yourself "I'll rest after the round"

The last one is the trap. The round won't fix it.

## Money runway personally

Founder wellbeing has a financial component you can't outsource.

- **Personal runway minimum:** 9 months of fixed expenses in liquid
  savings, separate from any company funds.
- **Re-check quarterly:** as your salary requirements change, re-check.
- **Inform your therapist** of your runway status — money stress is
  therapy material, not just business material.

If your personal runway dips below 6 months, that's a major life
decision moment. Either pause the company, raise faster, or take a
fractional/consulting income to extend. The decision is fine; the
denial is what hurts.

## What to NOT do

- Don't replace therapy with podcasts. Podcasts are entertainment,
  not therapy.
- Don't replace peer groups with Twitter. Twitter is performative;
  peers are private.
- Don't tell yourself "I'm fine, I just need to ship." That's burnout
  whispering.
- Don't quit Wander Women on a bad week. You make those decisions on
  good weeks.
- Don't compare your insides to other founders' Twitter outsides.

## Personal review — quarterly

Block 90 minutes, somewhere not your desk:

1. Re-read this file.
2. Ask: "Am I doing the things on this list?"
3. If no — what's the smallest change to do one of them?
4. If yes — what's working that I can do more of?
5. Update this file with anything new you've learned.

The quarterly review goes on the calendar. Same time, every quarter,
forever. Treat it as load-bearing as the cohort dashboard.
